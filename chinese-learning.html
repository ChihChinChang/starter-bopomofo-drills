Before You Begin

Learner's Guide — How to Make This Work

🧠 Your Brain Is Like a Rubber Band — Highly Plastic

  1. Your brain can always stretch further. Science calls this neuroplasticity: every time you practise, your neurons form new connections. No matter your age or background, your brain can learn any language. The only thing standing between you and fluency is repetition and the refusal to give up. Mistakes are not failure — they are the mechanism of learning. Make them boldly.
  2. Imitate, repeat, make mistakes. Learning a language is a process of imitation and repeated practice. Watch native speakers' mouths carefully. Embrace every mistake — errors are a sign your brain is stretching.
  3. Build up from memory. Once your pronunciation is solid, work toward recalling material without the aid of textbooks or notes. True learning lives in your head, not on the page.
  4. Listen first, speak second. When practising listening, mouth along as you hear. Fluency matters more than accuracy at this stage — you are building instinct and training your mouth muscles. Fill your life with Chinese: podcasts, films, conversations with native speakers.
  5. Study in quality blocks. Aim for at least one hour per session. Study until you feel bored, tired, or restless, then stop. No benefit in pushing past three hours.

Unit 3 — The Four Tones 四聲
⚠ Mandarin is tonal. The same syllable in different tones = completely different words. Tone mark appears to the right of the Bopomofo symbol.
— (1st)
陰平 — High Level
Hold a high flat pitch. Like a sustained musical note.
ㄇㄚ → mā · 媽 mother
ˊ (2nd)
陽平 — Rising
Rise from mid to high. Like asking "What?" in surprise.
ㄇㄚˊ → má · 麻 hemp
ˇ (3rd)
上聲 — Dipping
Fall then rise. Like a doubtful "hmm…" The trickiest.
ㄇㄚˇ → mǎ · 馬 horse
ˋ (4th)
去聲 — Falling
Sharp drop from high to low. Like barking a command.
ㄇㄚˋ → mà · 罵 scold

Q1
What is the Bopomofo symbol for the "zh" sound?

Q2
Write the Bopomofo for: b + a (1st tone)

Q3
Which tone symbol is ˇ — and what pitch movement does it make?
3rd tone (上聲) — pitch falls then rises, like a doubtful "hmm…"
Q4
What does ㄇㄚ (mā, 1st tone) mean vs ㄇㄚˋ (mà, 4th tone)?
ㄇㄚ mā = mother (媽) · ㄇㄚˋ mà = to scold (罵)
