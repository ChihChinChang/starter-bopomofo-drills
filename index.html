Before You Begin

Learner's Guide — How to Make This Work

These principles come from how language learning actually works in the brain — not how we wish it worked.

🧠 Your Brain Is Like a Rubber Band

  1. Your brain is highly plastic — it can always stretch further. Science calls this neuroplasticity: every time you practise, your neurons form new connections. No matter your age or background, your brain can learn any language. The only thing standing between you and fluency is repetition and the refusal to give up. Mistakes are not failure — they are the mechanism of learning. Make them boldly.
  2. Imitate, repeat, make mistakes. Learning a language is a process of imitation and repeated practice. Watch native speakers' mouths carefully. Embrace every mistake — errors are a sign your brain is stretching.
  3. Build up from memory. Once your pronunciation is solid, work toward recalling material without the aid of textbooks or notes. True learning lives in your head, not on the page. Memorise until you can perform.
  4. Listen first, speak second. When practising listening, mouth along as you hear. At this stage, fluency matters more than accuracy — you are building instinct and training your mouth muscles. The correct order is: hear → imitate → speak. Fill your life with Chinese: podcasts, films, conversations with native speakers. Even passive listening while doing something else counts.
  5. Study in quality blocks. Aim for at least one hour per session — more is better. Study until you feel bored, tired, or restless, then stop. There is no benefit in pushing past three hours. Come back fresh tomorrow.

The Bopomofo Sound System 注音符號

Bopomofo is Taiwan's official phonetic system. Master these symbols and you can pronounce any Chinese word correctly.

Unit 3 — The Four Tones 四聲
⚠ Mandarin is a tonal language. The same syllable in different tones = completely different words. In Bopomofo, the tone mark appears to the right of the symbol.
— (1st)
陰平 yīnpíng — High Level
Hold a high, flat pitch. Like a sustained musical note.
ㄇㄚ → mā
媽 = mother
ˊ (2nd)
陽平 yángpíng — Rising
Rise from mid to high. Like asking "What?" in surprise.
ㄇㄚˊ → má
麻 = hemp
ˇ (3rd)
上聲 shǎngshēng — Dipping
Fall, then rise. Like saying "hmm…" doubtfully. The trickiest one.
ㄇㄚˇ → mǎ
馬 = horse
ˋ (4th)
去聲 qùshēng — Falling
Sharp drop from high to low. Like barking a command.
ㄇㄚˋ → mà
罵 = to scold
